Membrane-bound Organelles
Independently Replicating
Mitochondrion (plural: mitochondria)

The “power supplier” for the cell, generating most of the ATP used in cell processes by converting nutrients into energy. Also involved in cell signaling, cell cycle control, cell growth, and cellular differentiation. Found in all eukaryotes.
Chloroplasts

A chlorophyll-containing plastid that converts sunlight and carbon dioxide into oxygen and sugar. Found in plants and algae.
Endomembrane System
Smooth Endoplasmic Reticulum

A series of sac-like membranes responsible for synthesizing and storing lipids, phospholipids, and steroids, as well as carbohydrate metabolism.
Rough Endoplasmic Reticulum

A membrane series studded with ribosomes, essential for protein synthesis and export.
Golgi Apparatus

Acts as a distribution center, gathering simple molecules to form complex ones, which are then transported, stored, or exported from the cell.
Plasma Membrane

A layer of phospholipids and proteins forming a barrier between the cell's interior and the outside environment.
Cell Wall

Found in plants, fungi, and some protists, providing structural strength, support, and protection outside the plasma membrane.
Lysosomes

A compartment containing hydrolytic enzymes that digest sugars, proteins, and other absorbed nutrients.
Nuclear Envelope

A double membrane surrounding the nucleus, forming a barrier between it and the cytosol.
Peroxisome

Contains enzymes for oxidation reactions, converting stored fatty acids to carbohydrates in plant seeds and participating in photorespiration in leaves.
Vacuoles

Used for storing nutrients, water, and waste. The central vacuole in plants provides structural support.
Vesicles

Spherical compartments that transport cargo between endomembrane system organelles. Specialized vesicles have diverse roles.
Cytosol

Also called intracellular fluid (ICF), it is the liquid matrix within a cell that supports organelles and facilitates intracellular processes.
